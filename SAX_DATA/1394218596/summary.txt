TITLE	Interleukin (IL)-33 with primary receptor ST2
BIOISIS_ID	ST2ILP
EXP_DESCRIPTION	Interleukin (IL)-33 signals through its ligand-binding primary receptor ST2 and IL-1 receptor accessory protein (IL-1RAcP), both of which are members of the IL-1 receptor family. Combined crystallography and small-angle X-ray–scattering studies reveal that ST2 possesses hinge flexibility between the D3 domain and D1D2 module, whereas IL-1RAcP exhibits a rigid conformation in the unbound state in solution. The molecular flexibility of ST2 provides structural insights into domain-level conformational change of IL-1 primary receptors upon ligand binding, and the rigidity of IL-1RAcP explains its inability to bind ligands directly.
SOURCE_LOCATION	NSRCC 23A, ALS BL 12.3.1
PUBLICATION	Structural insights into the interaction of IL-33 with its receptors, Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A. 2013 Sep 10;110(37):14918-23.
